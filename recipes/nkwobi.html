Nkwobi

About The Dish

Nkwobi is a popular traditional Nigerian dish, especially enjoyed in the southeastern region of the country. It consists of cow foot (or occaionally goat foot), cooked until tender and then mixed in a spicy, flavourful sauce.

Nkwobi is typically served in a wooden bowl and often enjoyed as an appetizer or a side dish, accompanied by drinks in social gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Onions 2 slice
  • 1kg Meat (chicken, wild meat, goat, cow foot, assorted meat)
  • 10 leaves of utazi
  • Edible Potash (one teaspoon)
  • 200ml Palm oil
  • 2 cubes of knorr
  • 6 peppers Scotch bonnet
  • 1 cup Ugba
  • 3 eeds of ehu (calabash nutmeg)
  • Salt to Taste

Preparations

  1. Step 1

    Season the meat with onions, two seasoning cubes and a teaspoon of salt. Cook for 45 minutes.

  2. Step 2

    Boil the Ugba for just 3 minutes with small water to eliminate fermenting bacterias

  3. Step 3

    Dissole the potash in one cup of water and filter into a pot

  4. Step 4

    Add the palm oil to the pot and stir very well, it should thicken and become yellow

  5. Step 5

    To the same pot, add grinded crayfish, Ugba, Pepper and Salt to taste.

  6. Step 6

    Add a seasoning cube, (utazi leaves, ehu, and peppers all pounded together)

  7. Step 7

    Stir together and add meat

  8. Step 8

    Stir and Serve

    Voila!!!
