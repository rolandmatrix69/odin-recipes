Egusi Soup

About the Dish

Egusi Soup is native to West Africa (Nigeria), and many of my American and Nigerian friends have enjoyed it. 'it is a great soup for those who like to try something different every once ina while. Ground Egusi seeds gibe this soup a unique flavor and color. If you can't find Egusi seeds, you can substitute pumpkin seedss. Anycmbination of crabs, shrimp and smoked fish can be used in place of the shrimp. Drained, smoked oysters and chicken can be used in place of beef.

Ingredients

  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 habanero peppers, seeded and minced
  • 2 pounds fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 18 ounces tomato sauce
  • 1 and half ups water
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 pound fresh spinach, washwd and chopped

Steps

  1. Step 1

    Place Pumpkin seeds in ablender, blend until mixture is powdery, 30 to 40 seconds set aside.

  2. Step 2

    Cut beef into bite sized cubes; season with salt.

  3. Step 3

    Heat oil in large pot over medium high heat. Cook beef in hot oil until brown but not cooked through, 3 to 5 minutes.

  4. Step 4

    Place omatoes, onions and pepers in a blender; blen ntil smooth, about 30 seconds. Stir tomato mixture into beef; reduce heat to medium-low cover. Cook until meat is tender.

  5. Step 5

    Add shrimp, tomato sauce, water and tomato paste; simmer for 10 minutes.

  6. Step 6

    Stir in Spinach and powdered pumpkin seeds. Continue simmering for 10 more minutes.
