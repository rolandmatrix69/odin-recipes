Jollof Rice

About The Dish

Jollof Rice is one of the most simple and easy to make Nigerian food, you'd want to start from here if you are new to Nigerian cooking.

Jollof Rice is the most popular food in Africa and, the required ingredients are abundant in most countries of the world. In Nigeria you can add fried meat, fried plantain or even vegetable salad

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of Rice
  • 1kg Chicken and Beef
  • 1000ml blended fresh tomatoes
  • 50ml blended fresh peppers
  • 1 Teaspoon Curry Powder
  • 1 Teaspoon Thyme
  • 1 Teaspoon Nutmeg powder
  • 4 seaasoning Cubes
  • 1 cup of sliced Onions
  • 300ml vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 Cloves of garlic
  • 2 fingers of ginger

Preparations

  1. Step 1

    Season the chicken/beef with a teapoon of salt, one seasoning cubes, half a teaspoon each of curry and thyme, half a cup of sliced onions then let it sit for some minutes.

    Set on Heat and let it boil for 10- 15 minutes, would take more time to cook the beef.

  2. Step 2

    Set aside the meat and stock in freh bowls. Peel pff the outer bask of the ginger/garlic, pound and grind

  3. Step 3

    Parboil the rice the normal way, boil about 7 cups of water in pot, add the four cups of rice and allow boiling for 3 minutes. Then pour out and wash thoroughly with clean water, then set aside in a bowl or plastic sieve.

  4. Step 4

    Pick out the parboilrd tasty meat and deep fry, set aside.

  5. Step 5

    Once you arer one with frying the chicken, reduce the oil to 250ml(1 cup) just add the remainng one cup of sliced onions, stir for 1-2 minutes, add the blended tomatoes. Preferably cook the jollof rice with the same oil I used while frying the meat.

    Using a fresh oil is just the same ascooking without the beef stock (meat water) which actually holds over 70 percent of the ingredient that is used in making most nigerian foods.

  6. Step 6

    Frying the tomatoes would take about 20 minutes

    Fry until when you lift a portion with your frying spoon, you will notice the oil continues to boil in the spoon.

    You'll also notice the tomatoes turning dark

  7. Step 7

    Once the tomato is well fried, add the meat water (chicken/ beef stock) to the fried tomatoin the pot, the blended ginger/garlic, half a teaspoon each of curry and thyme, salt to taste, add 4 cups of water.

    Add another seasoning cube. Stir and taste, it should taste overly spiced. Don't worry, once the rice is added the taste would balance

  8. Step 8

    Add the rice

    Then cover and allow to cook on low heat for 30-45 minutes. Remember that you can add water occasionally.

    Voila!!!
